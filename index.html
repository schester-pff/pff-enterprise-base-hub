Dashboard / Drop-in & 1-to-1 sessions

Drop-in & 1-to-1 sessions

Attending drop-in sessions and booking 1-to-1s are the best ways to accelerate your learning. You will get detailed answers to your questions and, in drop-ins, learn from the questions of other trainees.

Drop-in & 1-to-1 sessions

There will be multiple weekly drop-in calls led by members of the Base Training Team. The calls will cover a range of topics, mainly focusing on questions and difficult plays from the last completed training game.

There will also be 1-to-1 training sessions available with each Base Trainer. These will be calls where you will get personalized feedback and answers to your questions. The best 1-to-1 calls always happen with the trainees who are the most prepared; please come ready with questions and examples of plays to look at.

It is compulsory to attend at least one drop-in session or 1-to-1 session each week. For many of you, attending a drop-in session will be the much easier way to meet this requirement and provide you with more than enough information to help you improve.

1-to-1 training sessions

1-to-1 sessions are available from Week 1 through Week 3. They are not available during Week 0 (PFFU week) or your final test week. You can book one session per week, and need to do so at least 24 hours in advance.

You can find out more about the drop-in schedule and booking a 1-to-1 session in the #base-drop-in-1-to-1s channel on our Discord server.

Week 0 · PFF University self-assessment

Self-assessmentNo game yet

Welcome to the program. This week is all about working through the PFF University (PFFU) modules at your own pace and building a solid understanding of the data points you will collect for the Base process.

You should have received an email giving you access to the PFFU Training Portal on Thursday June 4th. The link for PFFU will be sent via email from datatraining@teamworks.com.

If you have not received that email by the end of the evening your local time on Thursday, please:

  • Check your spam and junk folders first.
  • If still missing, email datatraining@teamworks.com directly.

Please note: Your trainers cannot help with PFFU access or login issues — this is handled by a separate department. Contact datatraining@teamworks.com for any PFFU-related access issues.

Completing the Base PFFU Course

In Week 0, you must complete the Base course in full on PFFU by 9:00 am ET on Monday, June 15th.

Only those who fully complete this course will be eligible to move on to the trial games and the paid portion of the PFF Enterprise Academy Program.

PFFU must be complete by Monday, June 15th at 9am ET. You will receive your Game 1 information that day before 5pm ET.

Tips for using PFFU

  • The recommended approach is to download the Base Data Collection Guide from the first section, or via the link on the Dashboard of the Base Training Hub, and refer to it as you go through the videos.
  • After watching all the videos, read the guide in full to reinforce your learning.
  • You do not need to memorize the guide. Just try to get an understanding of what everything means and use it as a reference point.
  • The videos will take approximately 7 hours to complete.
  • You can rewatch any videos at any time.

How sections and quizzes work

  • You must mark each section as complete to unlock the next one.
  • You must score at least 80% on each quiz to proceed. Quizzes are not "wrong" — if you are getting questions wrong, revisit and review the training material in the videos rather than guessing.

We are monitoring your progress through PFFU. Once your course is complete, you do not need to do anything else to tell us you have finished; we will know.

Communication and drop-in sessions

The Base Training Hub and Discord are where you will find all the information you need to assist you in passing through our Base Academy Program.

It is vital that you check both regularly for announcements, including:

  • Drop-in session schedules
  • Additional resources
  • Q&A with your trainers
  • Asking questions in the chat rooms
  • Connecting with your fellow trainees

Optional drop-in sessions

Every day from Tuesday, June 9th to Friday, June 12th there will be optional drop-in calls led by Base trainers. These calls will be presentations focused on the materials you are going through in PFFU this week. If you want extra context to help you get through PFFU, these sessions will be helpful.

These are structured as class sessions, so they are 3 hours in the morning and 3 hours in the afternoon: 10am – 1pm ET and 2pm – 5pm ET.

These sessions will also be recorded and posted in Discord for later reference for those of you unable to attend.

Final notes

  • You will not progress to Week 1 if the PFFU Base course is not completed by the deadline.
  • Do not leave PFFU until deadline day — the platform gets heavy traffic and slows down.
  • If you have issues logging into PFFU, clear your browser cookies first — this solves 95% of problems.
  • Remember to mark each section as complete — nothing will unlock until you do.
  • It is vital that you have read all of the pages in the Base Training Hub and the information pages on Discord before you begin Week 1 of the program.

Common questions about PFFU

How do I confirm my PFFU is complete?
Go to PFFU Home → Menu Button → Universal Profile → Transcript → change the Active drop-down to Completed. If your course appears there, it is done. You do not receive a confirmation email. We can see completion on our end.

How can I go back on PFFU and watch the training videos again?
PFFU Home → Menu Button → Universal Profile → Transcript → Filter by Training Status drop-down: Completed → drop-down arrow next to "View Certificate" → Open Curriculum.

Dashboard / Rules and Expectations

Rules and Expectations

These are the rules of the program. They are straightforward and most of them come down to one thing: do the work, on time, to the best of your ability. Read them before Week 0. Being removed from the course is avoidable, and it starts with knowing where the lines are drawn.

Start here
Protect our material+

Sharing any of our proprietary material with anyone outside of the training program will result in your removal. PFFU, the Training DAT, the training guides, the game film, the training videos, the documents, the information in the web apps and the data are owned by PFF Enterprise / Teamworks and must remain within the program.

Getting things wrong is expected+

This is the most important thing to understand before anything else. Your feedback sheets will contain hundreds of errors, particularly early on, and that is expected. We are not looking for perfection. We are looking for you to follow the game, to genuinely attempt to enter the correct data, and to learn from your mistakes.

None of the rules below are about punishing you for being wrong. They are expectations you must meet to progress through the program and earn a position with us in-season.

What you have to do each week
Complete the full assignment+

Each week you must complete the entire assignment given to you:

In Week 0 — you must watch all of the Base training videos in PFFU and pass all of the assigned quizzes.

In Weeks 1–4 — you must:

  • Complete every play in the game you have been assigned
  • Fill in all the relevant data points for each play
  • Meet the deadline
What would an incomplete game look like+
  • Entering the same receiver/first contact player/tackler/etc on every play
  • Consistently failing to enter any of the location stats
  • A repetitive pattern of the exact same mistakes play after play, where it is clear you were not engaging with the game
  • Missing a deadline

Check that your play count matches the expected total when you finish your game. We will help fix your games for you, accidents happen. But repeated instances of the same issues in your game submissions will eventually be considered an incomplete game.

Deadline and extension+

Every assignment has a deadline of 9am ET on the Monday that closes the week as per the schedule. Do not leave your game to the last minute. Give yourself enough time to finish every play correctly and to fix any outstanding issue that needs correcting before the deadline.

You get one 48-hour extension+

Everyone is allowed one 48-hour extension. This can be used any time between Week 1 and Week 3. It cannot be used for the PFFU week (Week 0) or for the final test game (Week 4). Once you have used it, that is it, you do not get another. All extension requests must be made using the extension request form, available in the Base Hub and in the #base-forms channel on Discord. This must be submitted before the deadline. If you do not request it before the deadline, you will fail that week.

Drop-in sessions+

Each week there will be a number of drop-in sessions available to attend, you must attend at least one of them a week. Should you fail to attend for three weeks in a row you will be removed from the program.

Discord server rules+

There are several Discord channels you will be automatically added to upon joining our server. Each Discord channel will cover a specific topic; this may be a social topic or training-specific topic. Conversation must be limited to the topic of the specific Discord channel and must remain respectful and work appropriate. Engaging in heated debates and controversial topics will lead to your removal.

Spamming or promoting yourself or a business is not allowed. This includes ads, server invites and unsolicited DMs. Selling goods, services or any commercial activity is also not allowed.

Never upload files or content intended to crash or harm Discord or other people's devices.

As a collective group of passionate football fans, it can often be far too easy to be drawn into heated debates about a favourite player or team. These are not constructive conversations and have no place within PFF Enterprise. Those who consistently engage in this form of discussion will be removed from the program.

Harassment, discrimination, bullying, hate speech, offensive behaviour, NSFW content, violent, graphic or otherwise inappropriate content — this covers text, images, emojis, nicknames and links — will not be tolerated and will result in immediate removal from the program.

What removal means
You will be assessed at the end of Week 2 and Week 3+

At the end of both Weeks 2 and 3 the training team will review your progress to date. We will be evaluating your accuracy on the assignments, your improvement curve, your attendance and engagement in drop-in sessions and 1-to-1 sessions, your speed, and what your trainers have noted about your engagement on Discord.

If we do not think you are improving at a sufficient rate, or have not shown the expected attitude in the training sessions, you will be removed from the program. This decision will not be open to discussion and the training team's decision is final.

We will only let trainees attempt Week 4 of the program if they have met the expected standards. The trainees who are released each year are normally those who have struggled with the same fundamentals throughout.

This is not a surprise. If you are at risk, your trainer will have flagged it with you in drop-ins or 1-to-1s well before Week 2 or Week 3 ends. Take any feedback seriously when you receive it, and use the drop-ins and 1-to-1s to work on what has been flagged.

We do not just evaluate a trainee's standard of data work, we evaluate attitude to the training program as a whole and whether a trainee has met the standard we are looking for. We will flag any issues as and when they arise, but those who fail to listen to warnings risk being removed from the program.

Being removed — and what it means for your pay+

Removal from the program will be immediate. If you are removed you are not paid for that week, no matter how many plays you completed of that week's assignment.

How and when you'll be paid

Academy trainees who successfully complete a game assignment are eligible for payment for that game. All payments are made via Deel. Every eligible trainee will receive an email from Deel after the Game 1 deadline, with instructions to set up an account.

Week 4 is your final assignment and is treated as a pass-or-fail test. If you fail to achieve the passing grade required, you will have failed the final week and will not be paid for Week 4.

Dashboard / FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Quick answers to the most common questions. Still stuck? Ask Coach, or check the #base-faq channel on Discord before posting a question.

Pay & training structure
Is training paid?+
Week 0 is PFFU — an e-learning self-assessment that is unpaid. From Week 1 onwards, every training game is paid. The two half games pay $25 each and the two full games pay $50 each, so $150 in total across the four games.
When are we paid during training?+
You're paid twice during training — once after Week 2, and again at the end of the program.
What's the workload over the summer?+
Around 10 hours per week. The program is self-paced from home and includes both drop-in training call sessions and additional opportunities for voluntary 1-on-1 sessions with trainers.
What does "Week 0" mean?+
Week 0 is your PFFU self-assessment week — the prep week before the games begin. Week 1 is then Game 1, Week 2 is Game 2, and so on, so the week number and game number always line up.
Why is my deadline 9am ET Monday and not Sunday night?+
Deadlines are 9am ET on Monday rather than late Sunday night. The week effectively ends Sunday, but the Monday morning cut-off gives you a little breathing room first thing.
What happens during the rest week?+
There's a rest week for the July 4th holiday after the Game 2 deadline. No assignment and no new material that week — Game 3 lands when Week 3 opens.
What happens if I miss a deadline or don't complete a game?+
If you fail to complete a game, miss the submission deadline, or submit a game where it's clear no genuine effort was made, you won't be paid for that game and will be removed from the program. We take deadlines seriously — this is a professional operation and the data has real consequences for the teams that rely on it.
The work & the tools
Which DAT role should I select?+
Always select the correct Base data role in the DAT. Each week's page lists the exact filters to set as well.
Can I rewatch plays as many times as I need?+
Rewinding a play a couple times to catch the datapoints you missed is fine. But if you are watching a play numerous times to decide on one datapoint, it's best to make a decision and move on for the sake of speed.
How do I watch the game film during training?+
During training, the video files live in the Google Drive film folder for each game. Download the file and play it back in VLC media player — you can pause, rewind and replay as much as you need. Important: the video player built into the DAT does not work during training, so always work from the downloaded file in VLC.
Do I need a sports package or TV subscription?+
No. All the game film you need is provided in the Google Drive film folders — no TV subscription, sports package or external login required.
What software and hardware do I need?+
You'll need Google Drive, VLC media player and a Discord account. No specialist software beyond that, and no VPN. There's no formal hardware spec — a computer that can handle video playback and an internet connection fast enough to stream comfortably is all you need.
How long does a full game take?+
It varies, but experienced collectors often land in the 4–6 hour range. During training you'll naturally be slower, and that's expected — speed develops significantly over time, so where you start is not a concern to us.
Do we work individually or in teams?+
Individually. Each collector works their own assigned games independently.
Feedback & getting better
How is my work checked, and how do I get feedback?+
Each game you complete in training is compared against the same game charted by our training team. The copy yours is compared to is at least 99.8% accurate — effectively error-free. Anywhere your entry differs from ours, you receive an error for that play. Within minutes of submitting your game completion form, a feedback sheet is generated highlighting every error from that game, and you'll get an email letting you know it's ready. Go through it carefully to understand what went wrong, and note any plays or issues you want to raise with a trainer.
How much football knowledge do I need?+
A basic understanding of the game. This is a data collection role, not a football education program — for most people the learning curve is about the data, not the sport.
What separates trainees who excel from those who just pass?+
The ones who stand out treat training as a real performance environment, not a test to pass. They rewatch when uncertain, engage properly with feedback rather than just noting their score, and build consistent habits early. Those who rush through or plateau after initial feedback tend not to carry it through to the season.
How do I get the most out of training?+
Attend the drop-in sessions. Engage with your feedback — don't just look at the number, understand the mistake. Ask questions when something isn't clear. And treat every training game as a genuine performance assessment, because it is.
After training
Does completing training guarantee in-season work?+
No. Acceptance into training isn't a job offer — the Academy is the evaluation. How you perform across the program is how you earn your place for the season. PFF Enterprise operates as a meritocracy, and only those who excel will be brought on for in-season work.
Is there a path to a full-time role?+
Yes. Every full-time Data Analyst at PFF Enterprise has come through the contractor pool, and we hire full-time staff every year. It's not a guarantee, but it's a real pathway that multiple people take every season.

How to Use Discord

To get the most out of our Discord server, you need to ask your questions and have your conversations in the right channels. You will not get answers to Base training questions in the help or video channels — only in the #base-training channel. If you are having issues with video files or accessing your feedback, asking in #base-training is not going to get you an answer either; use the channel built for it.

If you have a question, check the #base-faq channel first and then use the search bar to see if it has already been answered on the server. The Coach chat bot, available from the Base Hub, will also have answers to a lot of your more basic course and Base related questions. Going this route first will often save you a lot of time.

When you do post a question, do not post it repeatedly if you do not get an immediate answer. Tag @basetrainer and someone will respond as soon as possible.

Discord is the place to get to know the trainers and your fellow trainees, and to get answers that help you understand the process better. It is not the place for answers like "which tackler & assister should I put on play 24" or "who got first contact on play 72". We will help you work out the answer, but we will not just give it to you.

The social channels are a place to talk sports. If you have an idea for one we do not have, reach out to Simon and he might add it for you. They are not the place for heated debates about your favourite player or team, and we expect everyone to behave appropriately at all times.

As an online company, our Discord server is effectively our office space and it should be treated with the respect that deserves. Behaviour that breaks the rules outlined on the rules and expectations page will result in your removal from the program.
